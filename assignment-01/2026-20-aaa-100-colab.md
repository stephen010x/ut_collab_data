# Accessing Google Colab Pro (Education Tier)

Google offers a **free 12-month Colab Pro subscription** to students and faculty at U.S.-based higher education institutions. This tier provides faster GPUs, more memory, and expanded compute units for your coursework.

### Prerequisites
* A personal **Gmail account** (The subscription is applied to your personal account).
* Your **university (.edu) email address** (Used for verification only).
* A valid **Student ID** or proof of enrollment (if automated verification fails).

---

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### 1. Navigate to the Signup Page
Go to the [Google Colab Signup Page](https://colab.research.google.com/signup).

#### 2. Select the Education Offer
Look for the section labeled **"Colab Pro for Education"**. 
* Click the button that says **"No cost to students and educators"**.

#### 3. Verify Your Student Status
Google uses **SheerID** to verify your enrollment. Follow these prompts:
* Select your **University/College** from the dropdown list.
* Enter your **University email address** (.edu).
* Provide your full name as it appears on school records.

#### 4. Complete the Verification
* **Check your email:** You will receive a verification link from `verify@sheerid.com`.
* **Upload Documents (if prompted):** If SheerID cannot verify you instantly, you may need to upload a photo of your Student ID.

#### 5. Claim Your Subscription
Once verified, you will be redirected back to Google Colab.
* Confirm the personal Google account you want the Pro benefits attached to.
* Click **Claim** or **Subscribe**.

---

### Tips for Success
* **One Account Rule:** You can only link your `.edu` verification to **one** personal Gmail account.
* **Logout First:** If you encounter errors, log out of all Google accounts in your browser, then log in *only* with the personal Gmail account you intend to use.

### How to Check if it Worked
1. Open any [Google Colab notebook](https://colab.research.google.com/).
2. Click on the **Runtime** menu in the top left menu bar.
3. Click **"View Resources"**. 
4. You should see a balance of **100 Compute Units**.
